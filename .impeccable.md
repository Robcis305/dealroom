# Impeccable Design Context — CIS Deal Room

> This file is the source of truth for design decisions. Any `impeccable` skill (`/icritique`, `/ilayout`, `/itypeset`, etc.) reads this before doing design work.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Active M&A dealmakers on both sides — buy-side and sell-side advisory professionals who spend heavy, sustained sessions in the viewer (hours per day during live deals). Bankers, principals, deal leads. They are sophisticated, time-compressed, and financially literate.

**Secondary**: CIS Partners advisors running the deal as neutral facilitators — they invite participants, organize folders, manage status transitions.

**Usage context**:
- **Desktop-primary**: office workstations, long sessions, high information density expected.
- **Mobile secondary**: quick checks on phone — "did they upload the financials yet?" "any new activity?" — scan and triage, not heavy work.
- **Emotional state**: due diligence is stressful and time-sensitive. Every second of friction compounds. Users are often reviewing under deadline pressure with executive stakeholders watching.

**Jobs to be done**:
1. Know immediately what needs my attention (new files, new questions, status changes).
2. Add, review, and organize files without thinking about where things go.
3. Trust that the other side sees the right things — and nothing they shouldn't.
4. Navigate dozens or hundreds of files without getting lost.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: powerful, legible, premium.

**Voice & tone**: Confident and operational. Never cute, never hand-holding. Speaks to sophisticated finance professionals in their own vocabulary (IOI, DD, engagement, closing). No emojis, no playful microcopy, no marketing language inside the product.

**Emotional goal**: After one session, a client should say *"This is the best dataroom I've ever used — easy to navigate, has everything I need to add files, review files, and tell me when I need to do something."*

**Combined energy**:
- **Confidence + authority** — "You're in safe, professional hands. This is a premium boutique experience, not a commodity tool."
- **Control + power** — "Bloomberg-terminal density. Everything is at your fingertips. Nothing is hidden from you."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Style**: Bloomberg Terminal meets modern SaaS. Deep dark base, dense information, sharp typography, brand red as a surgical accent.

**Theme**: Dark only. True-black-adjacent base (`#0D0D0D`), never pure black. Elevated surfaces `#141414` for cards/sidebars, `#1F1F1F` for interactive surfaces.

**Accent**: CIS red `#E10600` — used exclusively for primary CTAs, active navigation states, focus rings, and upload progress. Red is *earned*, not decorative. Status indicators use their own semantic palette (green/blue/yellow/orange/gray) distinct from brand red.

**Typography**: DM Sans (UI/body) + JetBrains Mono (data values — file sizes, timestamps, IDs). Note: DM Sans is on the impeccable "reflex fonts to reject" list but is **locked in by the CIS Partners brand spec** and cannot be changed. Accept this constraint and let the composition, density, and color do the distinguishing work.

**References (aspirational)**:
- Bloomberg Terminal (density, data-first, serious)
- Linear (crisp UI, considered motion, dark done well)
- Stripe Dashboard (clarity under complexity)

**Anti-references (what this must NOT feel like)**:
- **Dropbox / Google Drive / Box** — too casual, consumer-adjacent, not serious enough for eight-figure transactions.
- **Legacy VDRs (Intralinks, Datasite, Ansarada, Merrill)** — clunky, overwhelming, enterprise-gray, 2008 aesthetic. The user chose CIS *because* their incumbents feel this way.
- **Consumer SaaS (Notion, Linear, Figma)** — too playful or design-forward for M&A gravitas.
- **Crypto/fintech dashboards** — cyan glows, purple-to-blue gradients, neon accents are the wrong energy entirely.

### Design Principles

1. **Density with clarity.** Pack information, but make the hierarchy obvious. Nothing is buried. Nothing requires a second look.
2. **Earn the brand red.** Red appears only at moments of intent (primary CTA, active folder, focus). Decorative red is a bug.
3. **Navigation should disappear.** The user always knows where they are and where to go next. Wayfinding is the product's core value.
4. **The system tells you when you're needed.** Notifications, badges, and activity signals actively guide attention — users shouldn't have to check.
5. **Desktop-dense, mobile-legible.** Full density at desktop; on mobile, adapt the interface (don't amputate it).
6. **Motion is functional, not decorative.** 150–200ms transitions for state and panels. No bounce, no elastic, no parallax, no scroll-hijacking.
7. **Every character of copy is earned.** No redundant labels, no helper text repeating the obvious, no "Welcome!" banners.
